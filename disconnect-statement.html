Artist’s Statement – Disconnect

by Robert Fleek

Disconnect is an interactive fiction game created in Twine using the Harlowe story format. It explores the digital overload that many college students face — the overwhelming amount of notifications, screen time, academic pressure, social obligations, and the resulting impact on mental health. This game is inspired by my own experiences as a student navigating burnout, distraction, and the search for balance. I wanted to create something that felt honest but also playable, reflective without being heavy-handed.

The game takes place over the course of two full days in the life of a college student. Every decision you make — whether it's waking up early to help a roommate or staying up late doomscrolling on your phone — shapes your stats and affects how your story ends. Some choices are clear-cut, while others present more realistic, morally gray situations. The goal was not to create a simple good/bad binary, but rather to reflect how small behaviors compound into consequences, both healthy and harmful.

I designed Disconnect to blend humor, realism, and subtle horror. There’s a light, often sarcastic tone to much of the writing — especially in the student’s internal thoughts — but I also included a hidden horror route triggered by scanning a “forbidden” QR code. This unlocks a storyline involving a mysterious app called Signal, which builds tension throughout the game with glitchy visuals, eerie text, and unsettling passages. It’s a metaphor for how intrusive and unsettling technology can feel when it begins to control your routine instead of support it.

The game includes multiple endings that reflect different approaches to surviving college life: mindfulness, self-improvement, social connection, rest, total collapse, and neutrality. Each of these endings is designed to feel emotionally consistent with the choices that led there. For example:

  • CalmEnding: meditation and mindful choices
  • SelfImprovementEnding: high grades, structure, and personal discipline
  • SignalEnding: addiction to screens, obsession with Signal
  • TerriblePersonEnding: selfishness and indifference on Day 1

Technical Features

  • Over 90 passages
  • More than 5 gameplay variables (mental, sleep, screen time, GPA, social)
  • Custom CSS for dark mode, link hover effects, and glitch visuals
  • 10+ Twine macros (e.g., (if:), (set:), (link:), (text-style:))
  • Dynamic second-day content and side quests
  • Several distinct endings with their own visual styles

I also spent time styling the visuals to match the theme. The base game uses a dark mode color palette with teal accents, which makes it easier on the eyes and matches the calm-yet-digital tone. Signal-based passages use red, flickering text to visually differentiate the horror path. Each ending reflects its mood visually — soft fade-in for calm, bold for improvement, blur for rest, and gray tones for neutrality.

Ultimately, Disconnect is about awareness. It’s not a lecture on how to live perfectly or a gamified self-help tool. It’s more like a digital mirror — something that lets players experiment with their decisions, recognize patterns in their behavior, and maybe think a little more deeply about the systems we’re caught up in. I hope that players will find some part of themselves in it — whether that’s in the burnout, the attempts to improve, or the choice to simply log off and rest.

Thanks for playing.

— Robert Fleek
